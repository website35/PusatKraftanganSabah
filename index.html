About Us

Pusat Kraftangan Sabah is dedicated to preserving the rich cultural heritage of Sabah through traditional and modern handicrafts. The centre supports artisans, promotes creativity, and encourages cultural appreciation through craft-making activities.These products are the result of Yayasan Sabah Group's vigorous effort in training local people from all over Sabah. All these products are handcrafted by the handicraft trainees.

Our Services

Handicraft Production

Pusat Kraftangan Sabah focuses heavily on producing handicrafts that reflect Sabah’s cultural identity while also meeting modern market demand. The production process uses natural materials such as bamboo, rattan, wood, and plant fibres, which are commonly found in the local environment. These materials are crafted into products like baskets, furniture, decorative items, batik textiles, and traditional accessories such as beadwork. What makes the production system distinctive is that it is closely tied to training activities, where trainees actively create real products as part of their learning. The items produced are not only for practice but are also displayed and sold in galleries and retail outlets, making production both practical and commercially valuable. In addition, the centre supports sustainability by cultivating its own raw materials, ensuring continuous supply and reducing dependence on external sources.

Training

The centre provides structured training programmes designed to equip individuals with both technical and creative skills in handicraft making. These programmes cover a wide range of areas including batik production, bamboo and rattan crafting, and wood-based crafts. Training is comprehensive, as participants are taught the full process from raw material preparation to final product finishing. Beyond technical skills, the training also emphasizes design improvement, quality control, and the use of modern techniques so that traditional crafts can compete in today’s market. Many programmes run for several months, allowing participants to gain in-depth experience. The centre also collaborates with professional agencies to offer recognized certifications, which increases participants’ chances of employment. With facilities such as workshops, tools, and accommodation, the training environment is designed to fully support learners, especially those from rural communities and disadvantaged backgrounds.

Entrepreneurship Support

Pusat Kraftangan Sabah extends its role beyond training by helping participants become entrepreneurs. After acquiring skills, trainees are guided through programmes that support business development, including mentoring, product development, and marketing strategies. The centre provides platforms for artisans to sell their products through exhibitions, galleries, and tourism-related outlets, giving them real market exposure. This support helps bridge the gap between production and commercialization, allowing individuals to generate income from their crafts. In addition, initiatives such as craft villages encourage community-based businesses where artisans can produce and sell directly to visitors. Through these efforts, the centre helps transform skilled individuals into independent entrepreneurs, contributing to local economic growth and sustainable livelihoods.
